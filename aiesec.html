AIESEC Ace Project

Volunteer Street Marketing and Fundraising
for single mothers in Seoul South Korea

The Project

After first year, I really had my mind set on doing a six week international AIESEC working or volunteer internship. I was originally intending on going to Brazil but because of VISA issues, instead went to Korea. The project was originally to tackle the broader subject of gender related issues. We had a diverse group of interns from many different parts of the world. We finally decided that the mistreatment of Single Mothers was something we could all agree was a problem and also something that was extremely relevant to the South Korea.

Culture Shock

Before tackling any of the issues, I had to deal with facing an extremely different culture and one in which most of the population did not speak english. Part of the reason for these internationally oriented AIESEC internships was to engage issues from many different perspectives. As a Canadian, I was definitely more progressive than a lot of other countries but that wasn't necessarily a bad thing. As the topic was gender/sex related issues, we had people from conservative religious countries emphasizing chastity until marriage. Conversely, I had to try to explain the concept of Tinder.
